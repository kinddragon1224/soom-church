# Career Diagnosis Product Strategy Research — 2026-05-02

## Why this memo exists
Previous product/pricing suggestions were too intuition-heavy. This memo resets the product design standard around evidence-backed frameworks and testable assumptions.

## Sources scanned
- Strategyzer — Value Proposition Canvas: customer jobs, pains, gains, pain relievers, gain creators.
- Christensen Institute — Jobs To Be Done: customers “hire” products to make progress in a specific circumstance.
- GV — Design Sprint: answer critical business questions through realistic prototype + customer test before expensive build.
- Nielsen Norman Group — UX Research Cheat Sheet: research should happen now and throughout the cycle; different methods fit discover/explore/test/listen stages.
- Nielsen Norman Group — Don’t Listen to Users: watch behavior, not only stated preference; future-behavior claims are unreliable.
- Nielsen Norman Group — Test with 5 Users: small iterative tests beat large delayed research for usability discovery.

## Product design principles for this project

### 1. Start from a customer job, not a demographic
Bad framing:
- “20대가 29,000원을 쓸까?”
- “부모가 더 돈이 있으니 부모 타깃으로 가자.”

Better framing:
- “이 사람이 지금 어떤 진전(progress)을 사려는가?”
- “이 문제가 지금 얼마나 urgent/expensive/risky하게 느껴지는가?”
- “결과물이 어떤 decision을 도와야 돈을 낼까?”

Customer segment is secondary. The core is the job-to-be-done.

### 2. Separate three values
A diagnosis product may provide three different values:
1. Emotional relief: anxiety becomes named.
2. Decision clarity: options become fewer.
3. Career artifact: something usable for applications, parent conversation, portfolio, resume, job search.

Willingness to pay is likely lowest for generic emotional relief and highest when the output supports a concrete decision or artifact.

### 3. Pricing must be tested, not guessed
Do not assert 9,900 / 29,000 / 99,000 as truth.
Treat price as hypotheses.

Recommended test ladder:
- Free diagnosis: demand signal / segmentation.
- Paid beta offer A: 9,900 mini artifact.
- Paid beta offer B: 29,000 detailed report.
- High-touch offer: 79,000–150,000 session/report bundle.

But the test should measure actual behavior:
- Clicks on offer
- Form completion
- DM asking price/detail
- Payment intent or manual invoice acceptance
- Not “would you buy?” survey claims only.

### 4. MVP should test the riskiest assumption
Current riskiest assumptions:
- People trust Sun-yong enough from Threads + diagnosis page to submit personal career context.
- The report output is concrete enough to feel worth paying for.
- A specific job-to-be-done is urgent enough to convert.

Therefore the next MVP is not more web features.
The next MVP is a realistic sample report + manual beta sale.

### 5. Use manual delivery before automation
Before payment/AI auto-report/admin UI:
- Manually recruit first 3–5 beta users.
- Manually write reports.
- Observe what sections they value.
- Only automate repeated sections after evidence.

## Product hypotheses to test

### Hypothesis A — 20s / early career
Job: “Help me turn my messy experience into job-application language and choose where to apply.”
Likely output value:
- target role candidates
- resume/portfolio wording
- 2-week action plan
Risk:
- low disposable income
- may not pay for abstract “career diagnosis”
Positioning should sound like a job-search artifact, not counseling.

### Hypothesis B — student / parent
Job: “Help me understand my child’s direction without pushing the wrong path.”
Likely output value:
- child-fit environment
- parent intervention guide
- next exploration sequence
Risk:
- requires high trust
- parent may prefer direct consultation over report
Positioning should sound like parental decision support.

### Hypothesis C — 40–50s / transition
Job: “Help me translate my past work into a next option without feeling erased.”
Likely output value:
- career re-naming
- transition candidates
- avoid wasted retraining
Risk:
- needs nuance and trust
- may convert better to session than report
Positioning should sound like high-touch interpretation, not low-cost diagnosis.

## Recommended next experiment

### Step 1: Make one concrete sample report for each track
Do not price first. Show output first.
Each sample should include:
- fictional but realistic input
- current state one-line diagnosis
- real blocker
- two mistaken paths to avoid
- 2–3 direction candidates
- next 7/14 day actions
- when to request 30-minute session

### Step 2: Ask: “Which of these would someone pay for?”
Evaluate against:
- Is the pain urgent?
- Is the output concrete?
- Does it reduce risk/cost/time?
- Is Sun-yong’s trust asset visible?
- Does it create a next transaction?

### Step 3: Run manual beta
Use Threads/DM to recruit 3–5 people.
No automated payment first. Manual confirmation is enough.

### Step 4: Decide pricing after evidence
Potential pricing outcomes:
- If people only want lightweight clarity: 9,900 mini report.
- If people submit detailed context and ask follow-up: 29,000 detailed report.
- If people need judgment and reassurance: session/bundle first.

## Immediate correction to previous reasoning
The right question is not “Will 20s pay 29,000?”
The right question is:

> What urgent job would make this segment pay, and what proof/output makes payment rational?

Until we see real beta behavior, pricing should remain a hypothesis.
